ig doc project

view ->

i started the idea of documenting my life back in february, as a silent rant against curated content, where everything has to be aesthetically pleasing, even on personal life. thing is - not all the time a person has to face that way, the appeal is there but surely masks you off because it doesnt feel real in a way, its just not it.

so i recovered my ig account from 2023 and decided to take a photo everyday, black and white to bore people, even the mundane ones,everyday it has to be taken.

although as im writing this, i feel like the idea wasn't working, or is it just me? cause if this a documentary then i should be taking photo in the most random time, not when i feel like it?

also it seems like my perfectionism isnt really going far either, i always look back at my documentations and get concerned about the compositions, lighting, even if i don't have to.

or maybe im just not so consistent enough, i noticed when i make something specially when programming stuff, once i have the core functions working, i just keep going, i think of things in a most modular way - like when i built LDE from ground up and now im able to build apps using that core. now for documentary, its really hard to pull that off, maybe i dont have enough color grading skills (yes, even in BW shots has color grading) to make my photos consistent.

my roadmap

so lets say...

  • communications and socializing, not confident
  • app development, not confident
  • health and fitness, not confident
  • art and design, not confident

while im able to do them all, they all dont share same level, and that is totally normal for everyone. but i somehow have this mission where if im able to pull that off - i would be very happy, for myself. its a different kind of goal to achieve.

thing is, this is very very broad and dividing them to small chunks multiplied by how many areas i need to improve myself at - not so realistic. combining it with documentary seems like a good idea though, but only if im very disciplined enough

its not like im hitting the level of being good, thats so unfair compared to other professionals. what i meant when leveling all the bars is that when i feel like my skills complements with the other, and i feel happy about it

I want to build a life where all the things I care about fit together—where my skills, my routines, and my stories don’t compete, but complement each other.
